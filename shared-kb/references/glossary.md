# Cross-Functional Glossary

Terms used across multiple function apps (Procurement, Finance, HR, etc.).

**This is NOT a procurement-only glossary.** Procurement-specific terms (DoA, RFx, GRN, P-card, NFA, VMI, PAC, ASL) live in `proc-app/kb/functions/procurement/function-glossary.md`. Industry-specific terms (BF, BOF, EAF, slab, clinker, OPC, PPC) live in `shared-kb/industries/<industry>/glossary-industry.md`.

When a consultant hovers over a term in the app, the lookup happens across all 3 glossaries via cascade — most-specific match wins.

---

## A

**Annual Revenue** — Total income generated by a company in a fiscal year, before deducting costs. Used as the denominator for many procurement KPIs (e.g., Procurement Spend as % of Revenue).

**API (Application Programming Interface)** — A specification by which one software system talks to another. Relevant when discussing ERP integrations, vendor portals, payment gateways.

**As-Is** — The current state of an organisation's process, structure, or capability. Used in assessments to anchor the starting point before proposing change. Contrast with To-Be.

**Audit Trail** — A chronological, immutable log of actions taken on a system, document, or process. Critical for compliance and for justifying any analytical claim made to a client.

**Automation** — Replacing manual steps with system-driven execution.

## B

**Baseline** — The current measured state of a metric or process, against which improvement is compared.

**Benchmark** — An external reference value used for comparison.

**BU (Business Unit)** — An organisational sub-division.

**Business Case** — A document quantifying the cost, benefit, and risk of a proposed initiative.

## C

**CAPEX (Capital Expenditure)** — Money spent on long-lived assets.

**CEO (Chief Executive Officer)** — Top executive of the company.

**CFO (Chief Financial Officer)** — Top finance executive. In procurement, often the reporting line for the CPO.

**COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)** — The direct costs of producing the goods sold.

**Compliance** — Adherence to legal, regulatory, and internal policy requirements.

**Contract** — A binding agreement between buyer and supplier.

**CPO (Chief Procurement Officer)** — The procurement function leader.

**CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility)** — A company's commitment to social and environmental responsibilities.

## D

**Diagnostic** — The phase of an assessment focused on understanding the current state.

**Direct Spend** — Spend on materials and services that go directly into the product.

**DPO (Days Payable Outstanding)** — Average days a company takes to pay its suppliers.

**DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)** — Average days a company takes to collect from customers.

## E

**EBITDA** — A measure of operating profitability.

**Engagement** — One specific instance of an assessment for one client.

**ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)** — Integrated business software covering finance, supply chain, procurement, HR.

**ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance)** — Non-financial criteria used to evaluate companies and suppliers.

## F

**FTE (Full-Time Equivalent)** — A unit of headcount, normalised to a standard work-week.

## G

**GAAP** — Generally Accepted Accounting Principles.

**Gap Analysis** — Comparing the As-Is to the To-Be (or to a benchmark) and quantifying the difference.

**Governance** — The structures, forums, and authorities that oversee a function or process.

**Gross Margin** — Revenue minus COGS, as % of revenue.

**GST (Goods and Services Tax)** — Indian indirect tax.

## H

**Headcount** — Number of people in a role/team/organisation.

**HITL (Human-In-The-Loop)** — A workflow pattern where the system pauses for human approval.

## I

**Indirect Spend** — Spend not directly tied to the product.

**Internal Control** — Procedures that ensure reliability of operations, compliance, and accuracy of records.

## K

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** — A quantifiable measure of performance.

**KRA (Key Result Area)** — A defined area of responsibility.

**KYC (Know Your Customer)** — Compliance process to verify counterparty identity.

## M

**Margin (Gross / Operating / Net)** — Profitability measures at different points in the P&L.

**MD (Managing Director)** — A senior executive title.

**Maturity Score** — A 1-5 score representing how mature a process / capability / function is.

## N

**NPV (Net Present Value)** — The current value of future cash flows.

## O

**OPEX (Operating Expenditure)** — Day-to-day costs of running the business.

**Org Structure** — How an organisation is divided into reporting units, departments, and roles.

**Outlier** — A data point unusually distant from the rest of the dataset.

## P

**P&L (Profit & Loss)** — Financial statement.

**PAN (Permanent Account Number)** — Indian tax identifier.

**Plant** — A physical manufacturing or production site.

**Procurement** — The function responsible for sourcing and buying goods and services for the organisation.

## Q

**Quick Win** — A high-impact, low-effort recommendation that can be implemented in 0-3 months.

## R

**RCA (Root Cause Analysis)** — Identifying the underlying causes of a finding, not just symptoms.

**Recommendation** — A proposed action to address a finding.

**Roadmap** — A phased plan showing when each recommendation will be implemented.

**ROI (Return On Investment)** — Benefit divided by cost.

## S

**Savings (Realised vs Reported)** — Reported savings = what the procurement team claims achieved. Realised savings = what actually shows up in the P&L.

**Segregation of Duties (SoD)** — Splitting responsibilities across people to prevent fraud.

**Span of Control** — Number of direct reports per manager.

**Spend** — Total amount of money procurement processes.

**Strategic Initiative** — A high-impact, high-effort transformation taking 6-24 months.

**Supplier** — An organisation that provides goods or services.

## T

**TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)** — All costs across the lifetime of a purchase.

**To-Be** — The target state of a process / structure / capability.

## V

**Vendor** — Synonymous with Supplier in most procurement contexts.

## W

**WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital)** — A company's blended cost of debt + equity financing.

**Working Capital** — Current assets minus current liabilities.

- **Add** terms used across multiple functions (procurement + finance + HR + supply chain)
- **Don't add** procurement-specific terms — those go in function-glossary.md
- **Don't add** industry-specific terms — those go in industry glossary
- **Define crisply** — 1-2 sentences
